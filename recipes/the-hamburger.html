The Hamburger

Description:

The old reliable. With an entire plethora of different options to pick and choose from, you'll never be left hanging when it comes to desirablility.

Whatever you can think of, from buns to toppings, sauces, or cheeses hamburgers have it all!

In a nutshell, these sandwich variants are popular because they're plain delicious.

With so many places selling them for a decent price, and of course the hand-in-hand complimentary side of fries, it's no surprise why hamburgers are the most popular menu items ever!

Ingredients:

Have an instant-read thermometer on hand.
  • 1 and a half pounds lean ground beef
  • half onion, finely chopped
  • half cup shredded Colby Jack or Cheddar cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 (1 ounce) envelope dry onion soup mix
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • half teaspoon crushed dried rosemary
  • salt and pepper for taste

Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients and preheat an outdoor grill for high heat. Lightly oil the grate.
  2. In the meanwhile, combine ground beef, onion, cheese, egg, onion soup mix, minced garlic, garlic powder, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, parsley, basil, oregano, rosemary, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
  3. Use your hands and form the mixture into four firm patties.
  4. Cook the patties on the preheated grill until they're no longer pink in the center, and the juices have run clear. Around 4+ minutes each side.
  5. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 Fahrenheit (or 74 Celsius).
  6. Once complete, assemble your hamburgers. You're done!
