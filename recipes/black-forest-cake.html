Black Forest Cake

Description:

Black Forest gâteau, also called Black Forest cake, is a chocolate and cream cake with a rich cherry filling.

It typically consists of several layers of chocolate sponge cake sandwiched with whipped cream and cherries.

It is decorated with additional whipped cream, cherries, and chocolate shavings.

Ingredients:

Cake layers

  • 7 medium eggs, at room temperature
  • pinch of salt
  • 8 oz. (230 g) sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 and one-fourth cups (160 g) all-purpose flour
  • half teaspoon baking powder
  • 1.7 oz. (50 g) cornstarch
  • 1.4 oz. (40 g) cocoa powder
  • one-fourth cup (60 g) melted butter

Cherry filling

  • 12 oz. (350 g) morello cherries
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • one-fourth cup (50 g) sugar
  • 1.7 oz. (50 g) cornstarch
  • pinch of cinnamon
  • 2-3 tablespoons cherry schnapps

Soaking liquid

  • one-fourth cup (60 ml) cherry schnapps
  • 1 tablespoon water or cherry juice

Cream filling

  • 2 and a half cups (600 ml) heavy cream, cold
  • 2 and a half sachets whipping cream stiffener
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 sachet vanilla sugar

Decoration

  • 1 and two-thirds cup (400 ml) heavy cream, cold
  • 2 sachets whipping cream stiffener
  • 1 sachet vanilla sugar
  • 12 cherries
  • 3.5-5.3 oz. (100-150 g) chocolate shavings

Steps:

Baking the cake

  1. Line a springform tin (10 inches/26 centimeters) with baking parchment and grease lightly. Set it aside for now.
  2. Preheat oven to 375 Fahrenheit (190 Celsius).
  3. Divide the eggs, keeping the egg whites in a large bowl and putting the egg yolks into their own. Whip the egg whites with a pinch of salt until you get stiff peaks. Set them aside.
  4. Add the sugar and vanilla extract to the bowl with the egg yolks and mix until light and fluffy. This should take around 6 minutes.
  5. Mix the flour with baking powder, cornstarch, and cocoa powder and sift on top of the egg yolks mixture.
  6. Mix carefully, then add the stiff egg whites in, folding by several portions (folding is a mixing technique). Add the melted butter and mix it in.
  7. Fill the batter into the prepared baking tin and bake for 25-30 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean when inserted into the center of the cake.
  8. Take out of the oven and let it cool down in the tin for about 15 minutes, then remove carefully and let it cool down completely on a wire rack.
Note: At your discretion, you can bake the cake up to a day in advance.

Making it pretty

  1. Add the cherries intended for the filling into a sieve and let drain, collecting the liquid in a pot.
    • Add the lemon juice, sugar, cornstarch, and cinnamon. Mix in.
    • Heat up until the mix starts to thicken, then take off the heat and mix the cherry schnapps in.
    • Add the drained cherries and mix them in. Now set aside.
  2. Cut the cake horizontally into three even layers. Place the first layer on a serving plate in a cake ring.
    • Mix the cherry schnapps with water or cherry juice and brush it onto the cake layer.
    • Add the cherry filling on top - keep about 0.4 inches (1 centimeter) on the edges free of the filling.
    • Place in the refrigerator for about 15 minutes.
  3. For the cream filling, add the cold heavy cream to a bowl.
    • Mix the whipping cream stiffener with sugar and vanilla sugar, then add gradually to the bowl with the heavy cream, whipping constantly until you see stiff peaks have formed.
    • Add about half of the cream filling on top of the cherries in the cake ring and smooth out the top.
    • Add the second cake layer on top and brush this layer with the schnapps mixture as well.
    • Spread the remaining whipped cream on top and finish with the last cake layer. Brush again with schnapps, then leave in the refrigerator for one hour.
  4. Whip the cold heavy cream for the filling with stiffener and vanilla sugar until stiff peaks form.
    • Take about 6-8 tablespoons of that and fill into a piping bag with a star tip, and set aside.
    • Use the rest of the whipped cream and cover the cake with it.
    • Sprinkle the sides with chocolate shavings, decorate the top with 12 cream swirls along the edges, and place a cherry on each swirl.
    • Sprinkle the center with some chocolate shavings.
  5. Leave the cake in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours. Your Schwarzwälder Kirschtorte is finished. Congratulations!
